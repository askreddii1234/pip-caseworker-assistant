## Statutory framework

Schools are workplaces under the Health and Safety at Work etc. Act 1974
and are required to control exposure to hazardous substances under the
Control of Substances Hazardous to Health (COSHH) Regulations 2002.
Workplace Exposure Limits (WELs) published in EH40/2005 apply.

Specific to indoor air quality and chemical incidents, the responsible body
must:

1. Conduct and record a suitable and sufficient COSHH risk assessment for
   any process or substance that could foreseeably affect indoor air
   quality (cleaning, science practicals, art, DT, catering)
2. Provide adequate ventilation at the point of use, including local
   exhaust where exposure cannot be controlled by general ventilation
3. Maintain ventilation systems on a documented schedule; failure of LEV
   in regulated activities is a reportable defect
4. Investigate and respond to any reported air quality incident affecting
   pupils or staff

## Immediate response to a chemical exposure incident

Where a chemical release, spill, or strong odour is reported in an occupied
space:

1. **Evacuate the affected room** immediately. Move occupants to fresh air
   in a separate part of the building or to an outdoor assembly point.
2. **Do not re-enter** the room until ventilation has been increased and,
   where applicable, the substance has been identified.
3. **Contact the school office and on-site facilities lead** by telephone,
   not email.
4. **Call NHS 111 or 999** if any occupant reports breathing difficulty,
   loss of consciousness, eye/skin contact requiring irrigation, or if
   five or more occupants report symptoms.
5. **Isolate the substance** — close containers, label, do not move
   without identifying the contents.

The school must record the incident in the accident book and notify the
responsible body the same day.

## RIDDOR reporting

The Reporting of Injuries, Diseases and Dangerous Occurrences Regulations
2013 require notification to HSE where:

- A pupil or staff member is taken to hospital for treatment as a result
  of an exposure incident at school
- A specified injury (e.g. loss of consciousness from inhalation) occurs
- A dangerous occurrence — including the uncontrolled release of a
  substance liable to cause damage to health — takes place

Reports must be submitted within 10 days for over-7-day injuries and
within 15 days for diseases. Fatalities and major injuries require
immediate telephone notification.

## Cleaning products and ventilation

Cleaning operations should be conducted outside occupied periods where
practicable. Where cleaning during occupation is unavoidable, products
should be selected from a low-VOC range and the room must be ventilated
during and for at least 30 minutes after the activity. Products containing
hypochlorite (bleach) and quaternary ammonium compounds must not be mixed
and must not be used in poorly ventilated spaces.

A reported cleaning-product odour persisting more than 30 minutes after
the activity has ended indicates inadequate ventilation and should
trigger a ventilation review.

## Multiple-pupil incidents

Where five or more pupils report symptoms attributable to indoor air
quality in the same incident, the responsible body must:

- Treat the incident as a Category 1 air quality concern
- Notify the local authority public health team within 24 hours
- Commission an independent air quality assessment within 5 working days
- Brief parents of affected pupils with a holding statement within
  the same school day

Recurrence within a 12-month period of the same room or similar incident
is itself a separate notifiable event and warrants escalation to the
responsible body's executive team.

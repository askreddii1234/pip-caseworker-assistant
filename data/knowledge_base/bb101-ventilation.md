## Carbon dioxide thresholds in occupied spaces

CO2 concentration is used as a proxy for ventilation adequacy in classrooms.
For naturally ventilated teaching spaces, the daily average CO2 concentration
during the occupied period should not exceed **1500 ppm**. The maximum
concentration in any 20-minute period should not exceed **2000 ppm**.

For mechanically ventilated teaching spaces, the daily average CO2
concentration should not exceed **1000 ppm**, with a maximum of **1500 ppm**.

These thresholds apply to the breathing zone — typically 1.1m above floor
level for seated occupants and 1.7m for standing.

## Thermal comfort

The operative temperature in occupied teaching spaces should be maintained
within **18°C to 28°C** during heating periods, with a target of 19–21°C for
sedentary activities and 17–19°C for active areas (PE, dance studios). A
temperature exceeding 28°C for more than 120 hours during the occupied period
of a typical year is considered overheating and is a non-compliance.

Relative humidity should be maintained between **40% and 60%** where
practicable. Persistent humidity above 70% supports mould growth and dust
mite proliferation; below 30% increases respiratory irritation and risk of
static discharge.

## Required ventilation rates

Ventilation provision must achieve a minimum outdoor air flow of:

- **5 litres per second per person** in teaching spaces, with the capability
  to deliver up to 8 l/s/person on demand
- **10 l/s/person** in spaces with high occupant density (e.g. lecture halls)
  or where activities generate higher pollutant loads
- **3 l/s/person** in non-teaching spaces (corridors, reception)

Rooms used for activities involving solvents, adhesives or chemical fumes
(art, design technology, science prep) require local extract ventilation.

## Response to elevated CO2

Where measured CO2 exceeds the daily average threshold:

1. Open windows and doors to increase outdoor air supply
2. Reduce occupancy if structural ventilation cannot meet the load
3. Inspect mechanical ventilation systems for blockage, filter loading, or
   damper failure
4. Where elevated readings persist for more than two consecutive school
   weeks, the school facilities lead should commission a ventilation survey

Sustained CO2 above 2000 ppm during occupied periods is a Category 1 issue
and should be reported to the responsible body (academy trust, local
authority) within 5 working days.

## Mould and damp

Visible mould or persistent condensation on internal surfaces is a
non-compliance with thermal and ventilation requirements. The responsible
body must:

- Identify the moisture source (rainwater ingress, plumbing, condensation)
- Remove visible mould using appropriate biocidal treatment
- Address the underlying cause within a programmed period — typically
  6 weeks for minor cases and 13 weeks for cases requiring fabric repair
- Re-inspect at 3 and 6 months post-remediation

Where pupils with diagnosed asthma or respiratory conditions are taught in
the affected room, the response timescale must be compressed to 5 working
days for the initial mould removal.

## Testing and commissioning

New build and major refurbishment projects must demonstrate compliance
through an independent post-occupancy evaluation within the first 12 months,
covering CO2, temperature, humidity and ventilation rates across a
representative sample of teaching spaces.
